### 1. Depth-only and multimodal edge HAR

Edge-deployable human activity recognition using depth, Δ-depth, and confidence maps instead of raw RGB. Focus: Jetson-class hardware, latency–accuracy–energy trade-offs, and privacy.

**Keywords:** depth conversion from RGB datasets, Δ-depth as motion cue, early-exit logic, selective offloading.

---

### 2. WiFi + vision perception

CSI-based sensing combined with depth and other modalities to understand human motion and static objects. Includes empirical work on why static object recognition from CSI is hard and how motion-aware representations help.

---

### 3. Disruption-aware multi-view 3D reconstruction

Camera and edge nodes are treated as a portfolio of assets. Goal: keep 3D reconstruction quality high under camera drop, bandwidth limits, and varying network conditions using selection and scheduling strategies.

---

### 4. XR robustness and adversarial slow pose drift

Offloaded visual-inertial odometry (VIO) for XR can fail silently. This line of work looks at detecting slow drift and designing recovery or fallback strategies so that end-users still get stable experiences.
